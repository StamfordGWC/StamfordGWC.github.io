Know Your Rights

ICE Home

Link to youtube: https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=g1VwNVxb5K4

  • Don’t open the door unless they have a signed warrant by a judge
  • If they do have a warrant ask them to slip it under the door and make sure it is signed by a judge
  • If the warrant is signed by a judge, the person they are seeking should go out another door and go to them so other family members are not affected
  • ICE does not have the right to search your house without a warrant
  • Don’t sign any documents without a lawyer
  • When ICE asks you questions simply state that you do not wish to answer their questions

ICE Work

  • ICE cannot enter a public workspace without a warrant by a judge or permission from the employer
  • Communicate to employer and employees not to let ICE into workspace without a warrant signed by a judge
  • Remain calm, don’t run or give personal information. Let them know that you’re exercising your right to remain silent.
  • If there is no warrant, do not let ICE into the workspace.
  • If someone does let them in, they no longer need a warrant

ICE Street

  1. What to do if ICE approaches you on the street:
    1. Stay calm. Don't run.
    2. Ask if you are under arrest:
      1. If not: ask to leave.
      2. If you are not free to go, that means you are being detained.
        1. They can pat you down, if they think you have a weapon.
        2. If they search more you can state, "I don't consent to a search."
        3. Get enough information about the officers who arrested you. Ex: name(s), badge(s)#, etc.
        1. (After) the arrest:
          1. Ask for a lawyer and state that you will not answer any of their questions, "I will remain silent".
          2. Don't sign anything(s) without YOUR ATTORNEY
          3. You can make a phone call to your family to keep them informed.
          4. Ask for a hearing by a judge + bond
