Information for Recipients of Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals!

What is DACA?

DACA, or Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals, is a plan that the Obama administration put in place, so that children brought into the US with undocumented parents could attain legal status.

  • The children must meet several requirements and apply to be considered for the two-year defend action program.
  • Once you have received the approval of being a DACA recipient, you are allowed to have a work permit and protection from deportation.
  • Note: Those who are not DACA recipients, are not eligible to apply for these permits/protections.

Those who qualify and are granted DACA status are called Dreamers because the law that implemented DACA is called The Dream Act.

  • DACA recipients must apply every two-years to continue their guaranteed protection in the US.
